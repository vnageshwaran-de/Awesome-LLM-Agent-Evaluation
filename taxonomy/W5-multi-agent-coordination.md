# W5 — Multi-agent coordination

**Pillar:** I — WHAT is evaluated (Target Capabilities)

**Construct.** Performance where multiple agents must cooperate, negotiate, divide labour, or compete. Constructs of interest: communication efficiency, role specialization, consensus formation, robustness to a non-cooperative or adversarial peer.

**Measurement difficulty.** The environment is non-stationary from any single agent's perspective and outcomes are confounded by the capabilities of the *other* agents; credit assignment across agents compounds W1's credit-assignment-over-depth problem.

**Dominant failure mode.** Reward attribution ambiguity; verbose inter-agent chatter substituting for genuine coordination.

**Coverage note.** Under-instrumented with reproducible paradigms — a roadmap priority (Direction 5). Reproducible designs should control for peer capability (e.g., fixed, versioned reference partners).
