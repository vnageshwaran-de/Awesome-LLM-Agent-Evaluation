# W2 — Multi-turn reasoning

**Pillar:** I — WHAT is evaluated (Target Capabilities)

**Construct.** Maintenance and revision of an internal belief state across an interactive dialogue or investigation, including incorporation of new evidence and correction of earlier errors. Distinguished from W1 by *statefulness of inference* rather than ordering of actions.

**Measurement requirement.** Information distributed across turns and unavailable in any single one.

**Dominant failure mode.** Contamination by single-turn solvability — if the final question is answerable from the last turn alone, the multi-turn framing is decorative.

**Benchmarks tagged W2:** SWE-bench, GAIA, AgentBench, τ-bench.
